## Abstract

**Background:** The long noncoding RNA *IDI2-AS1* was recently identified in vitro as a negative regulator of interleukin 5 (*IL5*), a target of approved anti-eosinophil biologics, motivating an in vivo test in IL5-driven allergic disease.

**Methods:** We re-analyzed five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts (*n* = 856) covering asthma (GSE152004), atopic dermatitis (AD; GSE121212), eosinophilic esophagitis (EoE; GSE246323; GSE58640), and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis (CRSwNP; GSE136825) under a uniform DESeq2/limma pipeline and pooled by random-effects meta-analysis. Sample-level *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* Spearman correlations were re-estimated as partial correlations conditioning on eosinophil and Th2 marker-signature scores. In the largest cohort, a bootstrap mediation decomposition split the association into direct (ADE) and mediated (ACME) components.

**Results:** Bulk-tissue *IDI2-AS1* was invariant in every cohort (pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC = +5.1 × 10<sup>−5</sup>; *I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %; *p* = 0.47). Sample-level *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* covaried *positively* in every testable cohort, significantly so in asthma (ρ = +0.109, *p* = 0.004) — opposite to the in vitro repressive direction. Conditioning on the eosinophil signature attenuated the asthma association to ρ = +0.052. The asthma-cohort decomposition attributed ≈90 % of the covariation to the eosinophil compartment (ACME = +0.109, *p* = 0.002), with a direct effect indistinguishable from zero (ADE = +0.012, *p* = 0.76). Comparator lncRNAs and canonical type-2 cytokines showed the expected disease-stratified pattern.

**Conclusions:** Bulk patient transcriptomes do not recover the in vitro repressive direction. The pattern is compatible with masking of any per-cell *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* effect by eosinophil-composition shifts, although we cannot exclude the alternative that the in vitro repression does not generalize to patient cells; both possibilities require cell-type-resolved follow-up for adjudication. Beyond the *IDI2-AS1* case, the cell-composition-adjusted re-analysis pipeline described here provides a reusable framework for interpreting bulk-tissue null results for other low-abundance, cell-type-restricted lncRNA candidates.

**Keywords:** Asthma; Cell composition; Eosinophil; IL5; Long noncoding RNA.
