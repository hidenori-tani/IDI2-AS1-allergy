## Abstract

**Background.** The long noncoding RNA *IDI2-AS1* was recently identified as a negative regulator of interleukin 5 (*IL5*) in human T-cell-line knock-down experiments (Endo et al., 2025). Because *IL5* is the validated target of three approved anti-eosinophil biologics, an in vivo demonstration of the *IDI2-AS1* / *IL5* axis in patient tissue from IL5-driven allergic disease would have direct mechanistic and translational interest.

**Methods.** We re-analyzed five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts spanning four type-2 allergic diseases — asthma (GSE152004, *n* = 695), atopic dermatitis (GSE121212, *n* = 65), eosinophilic esophagitis (GSE246323, *n* = 10; GSE58640, *n* = 16), and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis (GSE136825, *n* = 70) — under a uniform pipeline (DESeq2 + apeglm shrinkage for raw counts; limma-trend for FPKM; *n* = 856 samples total). Per-dataset patient-vs-control effects were combined by random-effects meta-analysis (`metafor`). Sample-level *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* Spearman correlations were computed in each dataset, then re-estimated as partial correlations conditioning on per-sample eosinophil and Th2 marker-signature scores. In the largest cohort, a bootstrap causal-mediation analysis (1,000 simulations) decomposed the *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* association into average direct (ADE) and mediated (ACME) components.

**Results.** Tissue-bulk *IDI2-AS1* expression is essentially invariant in all five cohorts; the random-effects pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC is +5.1 × 10<sup>−5</sup> (95 % CI [−0.005, +0.005]; *I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %; Cochran's *Q* *p* = 0.47), a converging null across studies. Despite this, sample-level *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* covary positively in every testable cohort, significantly so in the asthma dataset (Spearman ρ = +0.109, *p* = 0.004) — opposite to the in vitro repressive direction. Conditioning on the eosinophil signature attenuates the asthma association to a partial ρ of +0.052 (*p* = 0.17). Causal mediation in the asthma cohort attributes 90 % of the covariation to the eosinophil compartment (ACME = +0.109, 95 % CI [+0.041, +0.176], *p* = 0.002), with a within-tissue direct effect indistinguishable from zero (ADE = +0.012, *p* = 0.76). Comparator lncRNAs (*MIR22HG*, *GABPB1-AS1*, *OIP5-AS1*) and canonical type-2 cytokines (*IL13*, *IL5*, *IL6*, *TNF*) recover the expected disease-stratified pattern in the same datasets, demonstrating that the *IDI2-AS1* null is gene-specific rather than a pipeline artifact.

**Conclusions.** The in vitro *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* axis is not contradicted by patient-tissue transcriptomes — it is masked by them, because cell-composition mixing in bulk RNA-seq dilutes the per-cell regulatory signal of a cell-type-restricted lncRNA below the noise floor and simultaneously generates a spurious positive co-variation driven by shared expression in the eosinophil compartment. The proper in vivo test of this mechanism is single-cell, not bulk. More broadly, the analysis illustrates why bulk-tissue differential-expression catalogs systematically miss cell-type-restricted lncRNA regulators of immune cytokines and frames *IDI2-AS1* as a candidate for cell-type-targeted RNA therapeutics in the *IL5* axis, complementing the existing antibody biologics.

**Keywords.** lncRNA; IDI2-AS1; IL5; allergic inflammation; asthma; atopic dermatitis; eosinophilic esophagitis; nasal polyposis; bulk RNA-seq; mediation analysis; cell composition; type-2 immunity.
