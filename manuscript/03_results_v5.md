## Results / Application

### Pipeline Step 1: uniform differential expression and meta-analysis returns a converging bulk-tissue null for *IDI2-AS1*

To demonstrate the first stage of the pipeline, we applied uniform DESeq2 / limma-trend differential-expression analysis followed by random-effects meta-analysis to five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts spanning four allergic diseases (Fig. 1; Table 1; total *n* = 856 samples). At the per-cohort level, patient-vs-control differential expression for *IDI2-AS1* was small and non-significant in every dataset (Table 2): log<sub>2</sub>FC ranged from −0.234 (atopic dermatitis; *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.41) to +0.246 (eosinophilic esophagitis, GSE58640; *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.15). At the meta-analytic level, random-effects pooling returned an overall log<sub>2</sub>FC of +5.1 × 10<sup>−5</sup> (95 % CI [−0.005, +0.005], *p* = 0.98) with negligible heterogeneity (*I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %; Fig. 2A). We emphasise that a low *I*<sup>2</sup> is consistent with — but does not on its own prove — a genuinely convergent absence of effect: near-zero heterogeneity can also arise when every contributing cohort is individually underpowered for a small effect, or when truly distinct tissue mechanisms happen to yield similar pooled magnitudes. What licenses the convergent-null reading here is not the *I*<sup>2</sup> value alone but its combination with adequate positive-control recovery in the same datasets: we recovered the canonical type-2 / inflammatory signature with the expected magnitude — *IL13* upregulated in atopic dermatitis (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +4.92, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 2 × 10<sup>−30</sup>) and EoE (Sherrill log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.91); *IL6* and *TNF* significantly elevated in AD; and *IL5* itself upregulated in asthma (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.42, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.014) and EoE-Sherrill (Fig. 2B). Read on its own, Step 1 would be filed by standard interpretation as evidence against in vivo regulatory relevance of *IDI2-AS1*.

### Pipeline Step 2: sample-level correlation reveals a sign-inverted bulk-tissue signal

The second stage estimated sample-level Spearman correlations between *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* in the four cohorts where both transcripts were reliably quantified (Fig. 3; Table 3). Contrary to the cell-line prediction of a negative association — the in vitro mechanism implies loss of *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* ↑, predicting an inverse within-cell relationship — all four datasets returned a *positive* Spearman correlation: ρ = +0.109 (*p* = 0.004) in asthma (GSE152004, *n* = 695, by far the largest); ρ = +0.489 (*p* = 0.054) in EoE (*n* = 16); ρ = +0.085 (*p* = 0.48) in CRSwNP; and ρ = +0.060 (*p* = 0.63) in AD. The directional inversion between Step 1 (converging null) and Step 2 (positive bulk correlation, significant in the largest cohort) is precisely the interpretive failure mode the cell-composition-adjustment stage is designed to address.

### Pipeline Step 3: cell-composition adjustment attributes the bulk-tissue association to eosinophil abundance

The third stage tested whether the inverted-direction sample-level signal could be attributed to bulk RNA-seq mixing effector cells (Th2, ILC2, eosinophils) co-expressing both transcripts with bystander cells expressing neither. We built per-sample eosinophil and Th2 marker-signature scores (mean *z*-score across 7 eosinophil markers and 6 Th2 markers; see Methods) and re-estimated the *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* relationship as a partial Spearman correlation conditioning on both signatures (Fig. 4 and Supplementary Fig. 1; Table 3). Adjustment attenuated the estimate in the three cohorts with a non-trivial raw association, abolishing it in asthma: the asthma Spearman ρ dropped from +0.109 (*p* = 0.004) to a partial ρ of +0.052 (*p* = 0.17); EoE ρ dropped from +0.489 to +0.425 (*p* = 0.13); the AD ρ collapsed from +0.060 to +0.010 (*p* = 0.94). In CRSwNP, which had no significant raw association to begin with (ρ = +0.085, *p* = 0.48), the partial estimate was marginally higher (+0.135, *p* = 0.27) rather than attenuated — as expected when there is no real bulk association to remove.

To formalise the contribution of the eosinophil compartment, the fourth pipeline stage — bootstrap effect decomposition — was applied in asthma (1,000 simulations; *IDI2-AS1* as exposure, eosinophil signature as the intermediate variable, *IL5* as outcome). We report this decomposition as a **statistical partition of the observed cross-sectional association under a linear model, not as a causal effect**: with single-timepoint bulk tissue samples (biopsies and, for asthma, epithelial brushings) the temporal-ordering and no-unmeasured-confounding assumptions required for a causal interpretation cannot be verified (see Limitations). Under this decomposition the total *IDI2-AS1*–*IL5* association was +0.121 (*p* = 0.05); the component statistically attributable to eosinophil abundance was +0.109 (95 % CI [+0.041, +0.176], *p* = 0.002), and the residual component holding the eosinophil signature constant was +0.012 (95 % CI [−0.075, +0.106], *p* = 0.76) — indistinguishable from zero. The proportion of the bulk association attributable to the eosinophil compartment was 0.90 under the marker signature. A sensitivity analysis for unmeasured intermediate–outcome confounding (medsens; Imai et al., 2010) indicated that a confounder strong enough to induce a residual correlation of ρ ≈ 0.60 would be required to drive this component to zero, so the partition is moderately robust to plausible unmeasured confounding.

**Robustness check with an independent deconvolution method.** Because a marker-mean *z*-score is a coarse stand-in for cell-type abundance, we asked whether the same conclusion holds under an independent, transcriptome-wide deconvolution. We estimated per-sample eosinophil abundance with xCell (an ssGSEA-based method modelling 64 cell types) and repeated both the partial correlation and the decomposition using the xCell eosinophil estimate in place of the marker score (Fig. 6; Methods). The two estimates of eosinophil abundance were concordant in the asthma cohort (Spearman ρ = 0.62). Conditioning on the xCell eosinophil estimate attenuated the asthma *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* correlation from +0.109 to a partial ρ of +0.074 (*p* = 0.051), and the bootstrap decomposition attributed +0.084 of the +0.121 total association to eosinophil abundance (95 % CI [+0.024, +0.143], *p* = 0.006; proportion 0.70; sensitivity threshold ρ ≈ 0.50). The eosinophil-composition explanation is therefore reproduced by xCell raw enrichment derived from transcriptome-wide signatures — an estimate independent of the seven-gene marker panel, though still an enrichment score rather than a calibrated cell fraction — with a more conservative point estimate: **between ≈ 70 % (independent deconvolution) and ≈ 90 % (marker signature) of the apparent positive bulk *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* covariation in asthma is attributable to shared eosinophil abundance, and the residual within-tissue component is indistinguishable from zero under both estimators.** We note one methodological caveat that is itself instructive for the field: xCell's default spillover-compensation step drove the *compensated* eosinophil score to its floor in the epithelial cohorts (41 % of asthma samples set exactly to zero, near-zero variance), which mechanically removes any signal to condition on; the *raw* ssGSEA enrichment, which retains a biologically plausible distribution (0 % zeros, median enrichment ≈ 490), is the appropriate estimate for a rare, spillover-prone cell type in epithelial tissue (Fig. 6D). Generic deconvolution applied without this check would have produced a spuriously null adjustment — a cautionary point for the broad practice of conditioning bulk analyses on off-the-shelf cell-type estimates.

The exploratory decompositions in the smaller cohorts were underpowered and mixed in direction (AD ACME +0.054, *p* = 0.15; CRSwNP ACME −0.028, *p* = 0.13; EoE skipped at *n* < 20 threshold), so the quantitative decomposition rests on the asthma cohort alone. We therefore restrict the quantitative attribution claim to asthma and treat the remaining diseases as underpowered context — their raw *IDI2-AS1*–*IL5* correlations are positive, but their adjusted and decomposition estimates are not uniformly directionally consistent — rather than as independent confirmation.

### Pipeline output: integration of Steps 1–3 into a single coherent reading

Combining the three outputs yields a single, internally consistent reading of the *IDI2-AS1* / *IL5* relationship in patient tissue that no single stage could supply. Step 1 produces a converging bulk-DE null; Step 2 produces a positively signed bulk correlation that inverts the cell-line direction; Step 3 attributes the majority (≈ 70–90 % in asthma, depending on the abundance estimator) of the bulk-level covariation to the eosinophil compartment with no detectable within-tissue residual. The synthesis is that the bulk readout is most parsimoniously interpreted as compositional masking — a hypothesis, since no per-cell signal was directly measured here: under it, any per-cell *IDI2-AS1* signal would be diluted across thousands of bystander cells in which neither it nor *IL5* is meaningfully expressed, while the small effector compartment that does co-express both transcripts simultaneously expands in active disease, which would be expected to generate the apparent positive sample-level correlation. This is the reading the pipeline produces from the three operations, without requiring the analyst to pre-commit to either a "no in vivo effect" or "in vitro effect generalises to tissue" interpretation. It remains an interpretation generated by the pipeline rather than an independently validated mechanism: the per-cell hypothesis it produces is the appropriate input to a future single-cell or in vivo test, not a substitute for one.

### Specificity check: comparator lncRNAs respond to disease whereas *IDI2-AS1* does not

As a positive control, we examined the three comparator lncRNAs that passed expression filtering — *MIR22HG*, *GABPB1-AS1*, *OIP5-AS1* — in the three symbol-keyed datasets in which they were quantified (Fig. 5A). Each showed clear disease-specific differential expression: *MIR22HG* strongly upregulated in AD skin (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.82, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 1 × 10<sup>−16</sup>) and EoE-Sherrill; *GABPB1-AS1* significantly downregulated in AD (log<sub>2</sub>FC = −0.50, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 5 × 10<sup>−3</sup>); *OIP5-AS1* modestly downregulated in AD (log<sub>2</sub>FC = −0.19, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 8 × 10<sup>−3</sup>). Pooled meta-analysis confirmed nontrivial cross-disease signal for the comparators (*MIR22HG* pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.46; *GABPB1-AS1* = −0.10; *OIP5-AS1* = −0.03). Against this background, *IDI2-AS1* returned the lowest pooled effect size (+5.1 × 10<sup>−5</sup>) and, uniquely, near-zero between-study heterogeneity (*I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %). We caution that the comparator panel and *IDI2-AS1* are pooled from different numbers of contributing cohorts (the comparators were quantified in three symbol-keyed datasets, *IDI2-AS1* in all five; Table 2), so the contrast in pooled estimates should be read as descriptive rather than as a formally matched comparison. With that caveat, the flatness of *IDI2-AS1* at the bulk-tissue level appears to be a property of this lncRNA specifically rather than a generic feature of low-abundance lncRNAs in allergic tissue.

### Specificity check: the type-2 cytokine network is appropriately disease-stratified

A complementary specificity check confirmed the analysis captures known disease biology in the expected pattern (Fig. 5B). *IL13* was the strongest disease-discriminating cytokine in AD (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +4.92, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 2 × 10<sup>−30</sup>) and elevated in EoE; *IL5* was significantly elevated in asthma and EoE-Sherrill; *IL6* and *TNF* strongly elevated in AD only; *IFNG* downregulated in asthmatic nasal epithelium but upregulated in AD skin, reflecting the known difference in IFN-γ involvement between airway and cutaneous type-2 inflammation. The cytokine panel behaves as expected from established immunology, providing a positive control against which the specific flatness of *IDI2-AS1* in the same cohorts can be interpreted with confidence. Taken together, the comparator-lncRNA and cytokine specificity checks argue against a gross pipeline failure as the explanation for the near-null bulk-tissue *IDI2-AS1* signal; they do not, however, exclude lncRNA-specific detection limitations in these heterogeneous public datasets, which remain a possibility we cannot fully rule out.
