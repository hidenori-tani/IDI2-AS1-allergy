# Cover Letter

**To:** The Editor-in-Chief
**Journal:** *Allergology International* (Official journal of the Japanese Society of Allergology)
**Article type:** Original Article
**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Manuscript:** *Bulk tissue transcriptomes obscure the IDI2-AS1 / IL5 relationship through cell-composition effects across four type-2 allergic diseases*

Dear Editor,

I am writing to submit the above manuscript for consideration as an Original Article in *Allergology International*.

This work extends our recent in vitro mechanistic study (Endo, Kurisu, Tani, *Biochem Biophys Res Commun* 2025), in which we identified the long noncoding RNA *IDI2-AS1* as a negative regulator of *IL5* in human T-cell-line knock-down experiments. The natural follow-up question — whether the same axis is detectable in patient tissue from human IL5-driven allergic disease — has direct translational interest, because *IL5* is the validated target of three approved anti-eosinophil biologics (mepolizumab, reslizumab, benralizumab).

To answer this, I performed a uniform re-analysis of five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts (*n* = 856 samples) covering four allergic diseases: asthma, atopic dermatitis, eosinophilic esophagitis, and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis. Three findings define the contribution of this manuscript:

1. **Tissue-bulk *IDI2-AS1* expression is invariant in every cohort** (random-effects pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC ≈ 0; *I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 % — a converging null across studies, not noise).

2. **Sample-level *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* covary positively** in every testable cohort (significantly so in the largest, asthma, *n* = 695), which is the *opposite* of the within-cell repressive direction predicted by our in vitro mechanism.

3. **A mediation-style decomposition** in the asthma cohort attributes ≈ 90 % of that covariation to shared expression in the eosinophil compartment (ACME = +0.109, *p* = 0.002), with a within-tissue direct effect indistinguishable from zero (ADE = +0.012, *p* = 0.76).

Bulk patient transcriptomes therefore do not recover the predicted in vitro repressive direction; the pattern is most consistent with masking by cell-composition effects, because the per-cell signal of a cell-type-restricted lncRNA regulator is averaged across thousands of bystander cells while the eosinophil compartment that co-expresses *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* simultaneously expands. To my knowledge, this is the first formally tested decomposition showing that cell-composition shifts are quantitatively the dominant explanation for why a published in vitro lncRNA → cytokine axis is not recoverable from clinical bulk RNA-seq, and it makes a generalizable case for prioritizing cell-type-resolved follow-up of in vitro-discovered lncRNA mechanisms in immune disease.

I believe this work fits the scope of *Allergology International* particularly well because the manuscript bridges three areas central to the journal: type-2 / allergic immunology (with direct relevance to the eosinophilic-asthma and CRSwNP axes on which anti-IL5 biologics act), transcriptomic methodology in human allergic disease, and the increasingly active field of non-coding RNA regulation of type-2 effector cytokines. The full analysis pipeline is publicly available on GitHub for direct reproducibility from the same public GEO data.

This is a single-author dry-analysis manuscript. The wet-lab mechanistic basis was published independently in *BBRC* 2025; the present study performs only computational re-analysis of public datasets, generates no new sequencing data, and required no new ethics approvals beyond those of the original studies. The manuscript has not been published and is not under consideration elsewhere, and the author declares no conflicts of interest.
