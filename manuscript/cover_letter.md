# Cover Letter

**To:** The Editor-in-Chief
**Journal:** Frontiers in Immunology
**Section:** T Cell Biology / Molecular Innate Immunity (suggest most appropriate)
**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Manuscript:** *Tissue-bulk transcriptomes mask the IDI2-AS1 → IL5 axis through eosinophil-composition mediation: a cross-disease re-analysis in four type-2 allergic diseases*

Dear Editor,

I am writing to submit the above manuscript for consideration as an Original Research article in *Frontiers in Immunology*.

This work extends our recent in vitro mechanistic study (Endo, Kurisu, Tani, *Biochem Biophys Res Commun* 2025), in which we identified the long noncoding RNA *IDI2-AS1* as a negative regulator of *IL5* in human T-cell-line knock-down experiments. The natural follow-up question — whether the same axis is detectable in patient tissue from human IL5-driven allergic disease — has direct translational interest because *IL5* is the validated target of three approved anti-eosinophil biologics (mepolizumab, reslizumab, benralizumab).

To answer this, I performed a uniform re-analysis of five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts (*n* = 856 samples) covering four allergic diseases: asthma, atopic dermatitis, eosinophilic esophagitis, and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis. Three findings define the contribution of this manuscript:

1. **Tissue-bulk *IDI2-AS1* expression is invariant in every cohort** (random-effects pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC ≈ 0; *I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 % — a converging null across studies, not noise).

2. **Sample-level *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* covary positively** in every testable cohort (significantly so in the largest, asthma, *n* = 695), which is the *opposite* of the within-cell repressive direction predicted by our in vitro mechanism.

3. **Causal mediation analysis** in the asthma cohort attributes ≈ 90 % of that covariation to shared expression in the eosinophil compartment (ACME = +0.109, *p* = 0.002), with a within-tissue direct effect indistinguishable from zero (ADE = +0.012, *p* = 0.76).

The interpretation is that the in vitro mechanism is not contradicted by patient bulk transcriptomes — it is masked by them, because the per-cell signal of a cell-type-restricted lncRNA regulator is averaged across thousands of bystander cells while the eosinophil compartment that co-expresses *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* simultaneously expands. This is, to my knowledge, the first formally tested demonstration that cell-composition mediation is the specific mechanism by which a published in vitro lncRNA → cytokine axis becomes invisible in clinical bulk RNA-seq, and it makes a generalizable case for prioritizing single-cell follow-up of in vitro-discovered lncRNA mechanisms in immune disease.

I believe this work fits *Frontiers in Immunology* particularly well because the manuscript bridges three areas the journal serves prominently: type-2 / allergic immunology, transcriptomic methodology in immune disease, and the increasingly active field of non-coding RNA regulation of immune cytokines. The full analysis pipeline is publicly available on GitHub for direct reproducibility from the same public GEO data, supporting the journal's open-science orientation.

This is a single-author dry-analysis manuscript. The wet-lab mechanistic basis was published independently in BBRC 2025; the present study performs only computational re-analysis of public datasets, generates no new sequencing data, and required no new ethics approvals beyond those of the original studies. The manuscript has not been published or submitted elsewhere, and there are no conflicts of interest to declare.
