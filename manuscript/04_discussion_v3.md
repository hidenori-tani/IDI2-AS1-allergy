## Discussion

### The interpretive problem solved by cell-composition-aware re-analysis

The principal contribution of this work is methodological. Standard bulk-tissue RNA-seq interpretation, as applied in the current clinical-transcriptomic literature, rests on two operations: (i) a differential-expression catalog comparing patients with controls, and (ii) sample-level correlation of a candidate transcript with its putative target across the same bulk samples. For protein-coding regulators whose function is broadly distributed across tissue cell types, this workflow is adequate. For lncRNAs — which are characteristically lower in abundance, often short-lived, and frequently restricted to small effector subsets (Mattick et al., 2023; Statello et al., 2021; Cabili et al., 2011; Tani et al., 2012) — the same workflow conflates two quantities that change in disease for independent reasons: the per-cell expression of the regulator inside its effector subset, and the abundance of that subset within the biopsy. The cell-composition-adjusted pipeline introduced here separates these two contributions using bulk transcriptomes alone, through a sequence of four steps — uniform DESeq2/limma differential expression with random-effects meta-analysis, marker-signature-based deconvolution of effector-cell abundance, partial-correlation analysis conditioning on the cell-composition signature, and bootstrap mediation decomposition of the sample-level association into mediated (compositional) and direct (within-tissue, per-cell-surrogate) components — that can be applied to any candidate lncRNA-target pair against any sufficiently powered public bulk cohort. All code and intermediate inputs (including CIBERSORTx-formatted matrices) are released openly. The framework is deliberately scoped to information recoverable from bulk RNA-seq, so that the large body of clinically annotated bulk transcriptomic data already in the public domain can be re-interrogated for cell-type-restricted lncRNA regulators without requiring single-cell or spatial re-sampling of the same patients.

### Pipeline behavior in the IDI2-AS1 worked example

The most informative property of the pipeline when applied to *IDI2-AS1* across five cohorts (combined *n* = 856) is the *combination* of two outputs that, taken in isolation, would point in opposite biological directions. At the cohort-summary level, the differential-expression and meta-analysis step returns a uniform null across all five datasets, with a pooled fold-change indistinguishable from zero and *I*<sup>2</sup> ≈ 0 % — the kind of converging null that a standard catalog operation would file as evidence against in vivo regulatory relevance. At the sample level, the correlation step returns a *positive* covariation between *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* in every cohort that supports the test, significant in the asthma cohort, which is opposite in sign to the in vitro repressive mechanism reported for the same lncRNA (Endo et al., 2025). The cell-composition adjustment then reconciles the two: when the eosinophil signature is held constant in the largest cohort, ≈ 90 % of the apparent *IDI2-AS1*–*IL5* association is statistically attributable to shared eosinophil abundance (ACME *p* = 0.002), and the residual within-tissue effect is indistinguishable from zero (ADE = +0.012, *p* = 0.76). Read together, these three outputs refine rather than refute the in vitro mechanism: the converging bulk-tissue null is what one would expect for a low-abundance, cell-type-restricted regulator whose per-cell signal is averaged across a predominantly bystander biopsy, and the directionally inverted bulk correlation is what one would expect when the biopsies vary in the abundance of the cells that co-express both the regulator and its target. The strongest evidence comes from the asthma cohort (*n* = 695); the smaller cohorts are directionally compatible but underpowered, and we treat them as such throughout. The pipeline therefore performed its intended interpretive function — it converted a pair of bulk-tissue outputs that would be filed in opposite directions by standard analysis into a single, mutually consistent reading.

### Why bulk RNA-seq is structurally blind to IDI2-AS1

Bulk-tissue RNA-seq measures a population-weighted average of every cell type present in the biopsy. For a regulator whose function is restricted to a small effector subset — here, presumably Th2 lymphocytes, type-2 innate lymphoid cells, mast cells, and eosinophils — two distinct quantities collapse onto the same readout: (i) the per-cell expression level inside the relevant subset, and (ii) the abundance of that subset within the biopsy. In disease tissues, both can change, and they do so in opposite directions for a putative repressor of a type-2 effector cytokine: per-cell *IDI2-AS1* would be expected to fall in active disease (consistent with the cell-line knock-down → *IL5* ↑ result), while the eosinophil/Th2 compartment expands. The first effect is diluted by the second, and a simple bulk mean, computed across thousands of bystander epithelial or stromal cells in which neither *IDI2-AS1* nor *IL5* is meaningfully expressed, is biased toward zero. The pipeline's cell-type-adjusted analysis dissects these two contributions explicitly: when the eosinophil signature is held constant, the residual *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* relationship in the largest cohort vanishes (ADE = +0.012, *p* = 0.76). One plausible interpretation of this pattern is that the bulk signal is dominated by composition shifts and that any within-effector regulatory direction is masked, rather than contradicted, by the bulk readout. This interpretation is also consistent with the absence of *IDI2-AS1* from previously published differential-expression catalogs of these diseases: the granularity at which most clinical RNA-seq studies operate is simply the wrong one for this specific molecule. Alternative explanations — including the possibility that the in vitro effect does not generalize from the cell-line context — cannot be excluded by the present data alone, but the parsimonious reading is that the in vitro mechanism survives the bulk-tissue test under appropriate adjustment.

### Generalization to other lncRNA candidates

The case made here for *IDI2-AS1* generalizes beyond a single transcript. lncRNAs are characteristically lower-abundance and more cell-type-restricted than protein-coding genes (Mattick et al., 2023), which means the per-cell signal of an authentic regulator is more easily diluted in bulk tissue than the signal of a typical mRNA. This implies that searching for "lncRNAs that are differentially expressed in disease tissue" — the standard catalog operation in clinical transcriptomics — will systematically miss regulators that act inside small effector subsets, even when their downstream protein-coding targets (here, *IL5*, *IL13*) are themselves easily detected as differentially expressed. The pipeline is directly applicable to other in vitro–characterized lncRNA regulators whose published mechanism predicts a per-cell direction that may be inverted in bulk by the simultaneous expansion of the lncRNA-expressing compartment in disease; the analytic decision to be made by users is the choice of cell-composition signature (eosinophil and Th2 here, but extendable to ILC2, mast-cell, regulatory-T, dendritic-cell, or epithelial-progenitor signatures depending on the candidate's expression pattern) and the choice of public cohorts large enough to support the mediation step. More broadly, the pipeline supplies an explicit re-reading discipline for bulk-tissue null results on cell-type-restricted candidates: a null result for an interesting lncRNA in a bulk catalog should be interpreted as "not seen at this granularity" rather than "not biologically active," and the cell-composition adjustment supplies a quantitative criterion for distinguishing the two. Existing single-cell airway atlases (Travaglini et al., 2020) provide the natural orthogonal substrate against which the pipeline's predictions can be tested for any specific candidate.

### Translational context

The worked example sits in a clinically tractable axis: *IL5* is the validated target of three anti-eosinophil biologics (mepolizumab and reslizumab, anti-IL5; benralizumab, anti-IL5Rα), approved for severe eosinophilic asthma and, in the case of mepolizumab, also for chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis (Bel et al., 2014; Bachert et al., 2017). The broader type-2 cytokine network is pharmacologically tractable in eosinophilic esophagitis as well, where for example anti-IL13 strategies have shown histologic and endoscopic improvement (Hirano et al., 2019). The clinical success of these axes is direct evidence that *IL5* — and the type-2 effector network of which it is part — is dose-limiting for the eosinophilic phenotype, which in turn implies that *upstream* regulators of *IL5*, including non-coding regulators, are mechanistically of interest. The pipeline's output for *IDI2-AS1* does not, by itself, establish the lncRNA as an immediate clinical biomarker — bulk-tissue expression is flat, so circulating or biopsy-derived bulk *IDI2-AS1* is unlikely to stratify patients — and it does not establish *IDI2-AS1* as a therapeutic target. What the pipeline does support is the prioritization of cell-type-resolved follow-up of this lncRNA in the *IL5* axis, in preference to further bulk-tissue profiling, and more generally illustrates that bulk-tissue null results on cell-type-restricted lncRNA candidates should be subjected to cell-composition-aware re-analysis before being read as evidence against in vivo relevance.

### Limitations of the framework and the worked example

Limitations apply both to the pipeline itself and to its application here. (i) The cell-type-adjustment step uses a marker-gene signature score rather than a deconvolution algorithm such as CIBERSORTx; we chose this for full local reproducibility from the same count matrices and provide CIBERSORTx-formatted inputs (`results/intermediate/cibersortx_input/`) for confirmation against the LM22 reference panel, but the marker-score approach treats the eosinophil and Th2 compartments as one-dimensional summaries and cannot resolve subtle redistribution within those compartments. (ii) The mediation decomposition requires a sample size large enough to support stable bootstrap estimates of ACME and ADE; in the present application this condition is met only in the asthma cohort (*n* = 695), and the directionally consistent but underpowered estimates in atopic dermatitis, CRSwNP, and EoE await replication in larger samples — users applying the pipeline to other candidates should expect the mediation step to be reliable only in cohorts of comparable scale. (iii) Because the underlying data are observational and cross-sectional, the ACME / ADE values describe a statistical decomposition under the assumed mediator model rather than a strict counterfactual causal effect; the pipeline's output for any candidate should accordingly be read as a quantitative re-interpretation of the bulk-tissue signal, not as evidence of causal regulatory action per se. (iv) We relied entirely on public datasets that were not designed for lncRNA discovery; library preparation choices (poly-A selection, ribo-depletion), sequencing depth, and read length differ between cohorts and may shape per-dataset detection of low-abundance transcripts. Related to this, the inclusion criterion required *IDI2-AS1* to be present as a non-zero feature in each retained dataset; the conclusion therefore applies specifically to publicly available cohorts in which *IDI2-AS1* is detectable at the bulk level, not to the broader claim that bulk RNA-seq always shows the lncRNA at near-null differential expression. (v) The meta-analysis pools log<sub>2</sub>FC across biologically distinct tissues (nasal epithelium, skin, esophagus, nasal polyp). Although every contributing cohort independently converged on a near-null bulk-tissue effect for *IDI2-AS1* — which is the substantive observation — pooled effect sizes across heterogeneous tissues are summary statistics of cohort-level convergence rather than a single biological estimand, and should be read in that light. (vi) The pipeline is bulk-only by design; it cannot replace, and is not intended to replace, single-cell or spatial transcriptomics. Its purpose is to extract maximally defensible interpretation from bulk data — the substrate that constitutes the bulk of publicly available clinical transcriptomes — and to nominate candidates for cell-resolved follow-up. (vii) The central biological interpretation in the worked example — that per-cell *IDI2-AS1* falls in active disease but is masked by simultaneous expansion of the *IDI2-AS1*-expressing compartment — is a hypothesis generated by the pipeline, not a direct measurement; the decisive experiment is single-cell or spatial transcriptomics of patient tissue from the same diseases. (viii) The present work does not retest the in vitro *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* mechanism, which was established for the HuT78 / A549 systems in the prior report (Endo et al., 2025) and would benefit from independent replication in additional cell systems; the present study asks only whether the published mechanism is recoverable at the tissue scale by cell-composition-adjusted bulk re-analysis, and concludes that it is — under appropriate adjustment — recovered as compositional masking of an otherwise consistent per-cell signal. Future work using the pipeline should (a) re-examine *IDI2-AS1* and its regulatory circuit in single-cell airway and skin atlases, (b) test whether *IDI2-AS1* knock-down in primary human Th2 cells phenocopies the cell-line *IL5* phenotype, (c) ask — in single-cell profiling of airway or esophageal tissue from patients before and during anti-IL5 or anti-IL13 biologic therapy — whether per-cell *IDI2-AS1* responds to therapeutic modulation of the type-2 axis, and (d) apply the same pipeline to additional in vitro–characterized lncRNA regulators that fit the low-abundance, cell-type-restricted profile.

### Summary

We have introduced a reusable, cell-composition-adjusted re-analysis pipeline that separates compositional from per-cell regulatory contributions to bulk-tissue RNA-seq signals for low-abundance, cell-type-restricted lncRNA candidates. The pipeline integrates uniform DESeq2/limma differential expression with random-effects meta-analysis, marker-signature-based deconvolution, partial-correlation analysis conditioning on cell composition, and bootstrap mediation decomposition; all code and intermediate inputs are released openly. As a worked example, we applied the pipeline to *IDI2-AS1*, a recently identified in vitro repressor of *IL5*, across five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts (*n* = 856) covering asthma, atopic dermatitis, eosinophilic esophagitis, and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis. In the largest cohort, the pipeline attributed ≈ 90 % of the bulk-level *IDI2-AS1*–*IL5* covariation to shared eosinophil abundance (ACME *p* = 0.002), with no detectable within-tissue direct effect (ADE *p* = 0.76), converting a pair of bulk outputs that would individually be read as opposing one another (a uniform null in differential expression and a positively signed sample-level correlation) into a single, mutually consistent reading in which any per-cell *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* effect operating in patient effector cells is masked rather than contradicted by the bulk readout. Whether the in vitro mechanism actually operates in patient cells will require single-cell or spatial transcriptomics for adjudication. Beyond the *IDI2-AS1* case, the pipeline is directly applicable to other low-abundance, cell-type-restricted lncRNA candidates whose bulk-tissue null results might otherwise be mis-read as evidence against in vivo relevance, and supplies an explicit re-reading discipline for the large body of clinically annotated bulk transcriptomic data already in the public domain.
