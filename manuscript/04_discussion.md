## 4. Discussion

### 4.1 Summary of findings

We tested whether the *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* repressive axis identified in a human T-cell-line knock-down system (Endo et al., 2025) is detectable in patient tissues across four IL5-driven allergic diseases — asthma, atopic dermatitis, eosinophilic esophagitis, and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis — using five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts spanning 856 samples. Three results stand out. First, tissue-bulk *IDI2-AS1* expression is essentially invariant between patients and controls in every cohort, and the random-effects pooled fold-change is centered on zero with negligible between-study heterogeneity (*I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %). Second, sample-level *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* abundance instead covary *positively*, significantly so in the largest cohort (asthma, *n* = 695), which is the opposite of the within-cell repressive direction predicted by the in vitro mechanism. Third, formal causal mediation in that same cohort attributes ≈ 90 % of the apparent positive covariation to shared expression in the eosinophil compartment, leaving a within-tissue direct effect that is statistically indistinguishable from zero (ADE *p* = 0.76). Together, these results refine — rather than confirm — the simple expectation that the in vitro axis would be visible as differential lncRNA expression at the tissue scale, and they identify cell-composition mixing as the specific reason the published mechanism is not recoverable from bulk transcriptomes.

### 4.2 Biological significance: why the in vitro mechanism is invisible to bulk RNA-seq

Bulk tissue RNA-seq measures a population-weighted average of every cell type present in the biopsy. For a regulator whose function is restricted to a small effector subset — here, presumably Th2 lymphocytes, type-2 innate lymphoid cells, mast cells, and eosinophils — two distinct quantities collapse onto the same readout: (i) the per-cell expression level inside the relevant subset, and (ii) the abundance of that subset within the biopsy. In disease tissues, both can change, and they do so in opposite directions for a putative repressor of a type-2 effector cytokine: per-cell *IDI2-AS1* would be expected to fall in active disease (consistent with the cell-line knock-down → IL5 ↑ result), while the eosinophil/Th2 compartment expands. The first effect is diluted by the second, and a simple bulk mean, computed across thousands of bystander epithelial or stromal cells in which neither *IDI2-AS1* nor *IL5* is meaningfully expressed, is biased toward zero. Our cell-type-adjusted analysis dissects these two contributions explicitly: when the eosinophil signature is held constant, the residual *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* relationship in the largest cohort vanishes (ADE = +0.012, *p* = 0.76), which is exactly what would be expected if the entire bulk signal were composition-driven and the within-effector regulatory direction is masked by — not contradicted by — the bulk readout. This interpretation is also consistent with the absence of *IDI2-AS1* from previously published differential-expression catalogs of these diseases: it is unlikely that a regulator of *IL5* has gone unnoticed for a decade by accident, and much more likely that the granularity at which most clinical RNA-seq studies operate is simply the wrong one for this specific molecule.

### 4.3 Implications for in vivo discovery of cell-type-specific lncRNA regulators

The case made here for *IDI2-AS1* generalizes beyond a single transcript. lncRNAs are characteristically lower-abundance and more cell-type-restricted than protein-coding genes (Mattick et al., 2023), which means the per-cell signal of an authentic regulator is more easily diluted in bulk tissue than the signal of a typical mRNA. A reasonable corollary is that searching for "lncRNAs that are differentially expressed in disease tissue" — the standard catalog operation in clinical transcriptomics — will systematically miss regulators that act inside small effector subsets, even when their downstream protein-coding targets (here, *IL5*, *IL13*) are themselves easily detected as differentially expressed. The *IDI2-AS1* / *IL5* contrast is therefore a useful illustration of why future discovery of in vivo lncRNA function in immune disease should preferentially use single-cell or spatial assays, and why a null result for an interesting lncRNA in a bulk catalog should be interpreted as "not seen at this granularity" rather than "not biologically active." Existing single-cell asthma atlases (e.g. Sajuthi et al., 2023; Travaglini et al., 2020) provide the natural follow-up substrate: testing whether *IDI2-AS1* is enriched in eosinophils, ILC2s, or Th2 cells in patient airway tissue, and whether its per-cell expression in those subsets falls in active disease, would be the directly informative experiment.

### 4.4 Clinical and translational context

*IL5* is the validated target of three anti-eosinophil biologics (mepolizumab and reslizumab, anti-IL5; benralizumab, anti-IL5Rα), all approved for severe eosinophilic asthma and, increasingly, for related type-2 indications including EoE and CRSwNP (Bel et al., 2014; Bachert et al., 2019; Hirano et al., 2019). The clinical success of this axis is direct evidence that *IL5* is dose-limiting for the eosinophilic phenotype, which in turn implies that any *upstream* regulator of *IL5* — including a non-coding regulator — is mechanistically of interest. Our results do not, by themselves, support *IDI2-AS1* as an immediate clinical biomarker: tissue-bulk expression is flat, so circulating or biopsy-derived bulk *IDI2-AS1* is unlikely to stratify patients. They do, however, position the lncRNA as a candidate in the rapidly maturing field of cell-type-targeted RNA therapeutics: an oligonucleotide-based modulator delivered to Th2/eosinophil lineages would have a defined molecular target whose downstream relevance has already been validated by the existing biologics. In this framing the present manuscript serves as the in vivo-context companion to the earlier in vitro mechanistic report and as a brief against over-interpreting bulk-tissue null results when the candidate molecule is cell-type-restricted.

### 4.5 Limitations and future directions

Several caveats accompany this analysis. First, we relied entirely on public datasets that were not designed for lncRNA discovery; library preparation choices (poly-A selection, ribo-depletion), sequencing depth, and read length differ between cohorts and may shape the per-dataset detection of low-abundance transcripts even when an effect would otherwise be present. Second, the cell-type-adjustment uses a marker-gene signature score rather than a deconvolution algorithm such as CIBERSORTx. We chose this approach for full local reproducibility from the same count matrices, and we provide CIBERSORTx-formatted inputs (`results/intermediate/cibersortx_input/`) so that readers can confirm the conclusion with the LM22 reference panel; nonetheless, the marker-score approach treats the eosinophil and Th2 compartments as one-dimensional summaries and cannot resolve subtle redistribution within those compartments. Third, the formal mediation analysis is well-powered only in the asthma cohort (*n* = 695); the directionally consistent but underpowered estimates in atopic dermatitis, CRSwNP, and EoE call for replication in larger samples. Fourth, the central biological interpretation — that per-cell *IDI2-AS1* falls in active disease but is masked by simultaneous expansion of the *IDI2-AS1*-expressing compartment — is a hypothesis derived from the bulk data, not a direct measurement; the decisive experiment is single-cell or spatial transcriptomics of patient tissue from the same diseases. Finally, the present work does not test the in vitro mechanism itself, which has been independently established for the HuT78 / A549 systems by our prior study; it asks only whether that mechanism manifests at the tissue scale, and concludes that it does not, for reasons that are themselves biologically informative. Future work should (i) re-examine *IDI2-AS1* and its regulatory circuit in single-cell airway and skin atlases, (ii) test whether *IDI2-AS1* knock-down in primary human Th2 cells phenocopies the cell-line *IL5* phenotype, and (iii) explore whether oligonucleotide-based modulation of *IDI2-AS1* in cell-type-targeted delivery systems can recapitulate the IL5-axis attenuation that anti-IL5 biologics achieve through the protein-level pathway.
