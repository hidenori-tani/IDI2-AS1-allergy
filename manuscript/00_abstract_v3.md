## Abstract

**Background.** Long noncoding RNAs (lncRNAs) are characteristically low in abundance and restricted to small cell subsets, with the consequence that authentic cell-type-specific regulators of disease can be effectively invisible in standard bulk-tissue RNA-seq catalogs. When the per-cell effect of such a regulator and the abundance of the cell population that expresses it move in opposite directions within a diseased biopsy, the bulk readout systematically conflates compositional and regulatory components, and a true per-cell effect may be masked, inverted, or filed as a null result. No standardized framework currently separates these two contributions from bulk transcriptomes alone.

**Approach.** We present a reusable, cell-composition-adjusted re-analysis pipeline that combines (i) uniform DESeq2/limma differential-expression analysis with random-effects meta-analysis across heterogeneous cohorts, (ii) marker-signature-based deconvolution of effector-cell abundance (with CIBERSORTx-compatible inputs released), (iii) partial-correlation analysis conditioning on the relevant cell-composition signature, and (iv) bootstrap mediation decomposition into average direct (ADE) and mediated (ACME) components. All code and intermediate outputs are released openly.

**Worked example.** We applied the pipeline to *IDI2-AS1*, a recently identified in vitro repressor of *IL5*, across five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts (*n* = 856) covering four IL5-driven type-2 allergic diseases (asthma, atopic dermatitis, eosinophilic esophagitis, chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis). Bulk *IDI2-AS1* was invariant in every cohort (pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC ≈ 0; *I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %). Sample-level *IDI2-AS1* covaried positively with *IL5* — opposite to the in vitro repressive direction. Cell-composition adjustment in the largest cohort attributed ≈ 90 % of the covariation to shared eosinophil abundance (ACME *p* = 0.002), with no detectable direct effect (ADE *p* = 0.76).

**Implications.** The pipeline demonstrates how cell-composition-adjusted re-analysis can prevent the systematic mis-reading of bulk-tissue null results as evidence against cell-type-restricted lncRNA function. For *IDI2-AS1*, the bulk-tissue readout is most parsimoniously explained as compositional masking rather than as evidence against in vivo relevance, with single-cell follow-up required for adjudication. The framework is directly applicable to other low-abundance lncRNA candidates evaluated against public bulk cohorts.

**Keywords:** long noncoding RNA; bulk RNA-seq; cell-composition deconvolution; random-effects meta-analysis; partial correlation; mediation analysis; IL5; type-2 inflammation.
