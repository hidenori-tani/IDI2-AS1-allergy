## 3. Results

### 3.1 Tissue-bulk *IDI2-AS1* expression is invariant across four IL5-driven allergic diseases

To test whether the in vitro *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* repressive axis described in our recent T-cell-line study (Endo et al., 2025) is reflected in patient tissues, we re-analyzed five public bulk RNA-seq cohorts spanning four allergic diseases (Table 1; total *n* = 856 samples). Per-dataset patient-vs-control differential expression for *IDI2-AS1* yielded effect sizes that were small in every cohort and statistically significant in none: log<sub>2</sub>FC ranged from −0.234 (atopic dermatitis, GSE121212; *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.41) to +0.247 (eosinophilic esophagitis, GSE58640; *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.15), with intermediate values in the asthma (+0.0015), CRSwNP (−2.8 × 10<sup>−6</sup>), and EoE-Kleuskens (−0.036) cohorts. A random-effects meta-analysis pooling all five datasets returned an overall log<sub>2</sub>FC of +5.1 × 10<sup>−5</sup> (95 % CI [−0.005, +0.005], *p* = 0.98) with negligible heterogeneity (*I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %, Cochran's *Q* *p* = 0.47; Figure 2A). Importantly, this null is not a failure of the pipeline: in the same datasets we recovered the canonical type-2 / inflammatory signature with the expected direction and magnitude — *IL13* was strongly upregulated in atopic dermatitis (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +4.92, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 2 × 10<sup>−30</sup>) and in EoE (Sherrill, log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.91, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 2 × 10<sup>−5</sup>); *IL6* and *TNF* were significantly elevated in AD (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +3.25 and +0.89; both *p*<sub>adj</sub> < 10<sup>−7</sup>); and *IL5* itself was upregulated in asthma (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.42, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.014) and EoE-Sherrill (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.61, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.002). The five-dataset *I*<sup>2</sup> ≈ 0 % observation for *IDI2-AS1* therefore reflects a robust convergent finding — *all* cohorts independently report no tissue-bulk effect — rather than noise or pipeline artifact.

### 3.2 *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* covary positively at the tissue-bulk level — opposite to the cell-line prediction

We next asked whether the within-cell repressive relationship between *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* documented in Endo et al. (2025) is detectable as a sample-level correlation in the four cohorts where both transcripts are reliably quantified (Figure 3, Table 2). Contrary to the cell-line prediction of a negative association, all four datasets returned a *positive* Spearman correlation between *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5*: ρ = +0.109 (*p* = 0.004) in the asthma cohort (GSE152004, *n* = 695 — by far the largest and best-powered); ρ = +0.489 (*p* = 0.054) in EoE (GSE58640, *n* = 16); ρ = +0.085 (*p* = 0.48) in CRSwNP; and ρ = +0.060 (*p* = 0.63) in atopic dermatitis. The directional inversion between the published in vitro mechanism (knock-down → IL5 ↑, predicting negative in-tissue correlation) and the in vivo bulk readout (positive correlation, significant in the largest cohort) is the central observation that motivates the cell-composition analysis of the next section.

### 3.3 The tissue-level *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* association is mediated by eosinophil composition

A natural explanation for the inverted-direction tissue signal is that bulk RNA-seq mixes effector cells in which both *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* are co-expressed (Th2 lymphocytes, ILC2s, eosinophils) with bystander tissue cells in which neither is expressed; samples with greater allergic infiltration would then carry up *both* transcripts in proportion, producing a spurious positive correlation that is driven by composition rather than within-cell regulation. To test this directly, we built per-sample eosinophil and Th2 marker-signature scores (mean *z*-score across 7 eosinophil markers and 6 Th2 markers; Methods §2.4) and re-estimated the *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* relationship as a partial Spearman correlation conditioning on both signatures (Table 2, Figure 4). Adjustment substantially attenuated the raw association in every cohort and abolished it in the asthma dataset where it had reached significance: the asthma Spearman ρ dropped from +0.109 (*p* = 0.004) to a partial ρ of +0.052 (*p* = 0.17), the EoE ρ dropped from +0.489 to +0.425 (*p* = 0.13), the CRSwNP ρ moved slightly from +0.085 to +0.135 (*p* = 0.27), and the AD ρ collapsed from +0.060 to +0.010 (*p* = 0.94). To formalize the contribution of the eosinophil compartment specifically, we performed a bootstrap causal-mediation analysis in the asthma cohort (1 000 simulations; *IDI2-AS1* as treatment, eosinophil signature as mediator, *IL5* as outcome). The average causal mediation effect through eosinophil composition was ACME = +0.109 (95 % CI [+0.041, +0.176], *p* = 0.002), while the average direct effect — the within-tissue residual *IDI2-AS1* → *IL5* association after holding the eosinophil signature constant — was ADE = +0.012 (95 % CI [−0.075, +0.106], *p* = 0.76); the proportion mediated was 0.90. In other words, ≈ 90 % of the apparent positive *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* covariation in the largest available patient cohort is statistically attributable to shared eosinophil abundance, and the residual within-tissue direct effect is indistinguishable from zero. Mediation in the smaller cohorts was directionally consistent but underpowered (atopic dermatitis ACME = +0.054, *p* = 0.10; CRSwNP ACME = −0.028, *p* = 0.13; EoE-Sherrill skipped because *n* = 16 falls below the prespecified 20-sample mediation threshold).

### 3.4 Specificity: comparator lncRNAs respond to disease whereas *IDI2-AS1* does not

To test whether the flat tissue-bulk profile of *IDI2-AS1* is a genuine biological feature or a generic property of all expressed lncRNAs in these tissues, we examined four comparator lncRNAs — *MIR22HG*, *GABPB1-AS1*, *OIP5-AS1*, and *LITATS1* — across the same five datasets (Figure 5). Three of the four comparators showed clear, disease-specific differential expression. *MIR22HG* was strongly upregulated in atopic-dermatitis skin (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.82, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 5 × 10<sup>−18</sup>) but moved little in the other tissues. *GABPB1-AS1* was downregulated in three of five cohorts, reaching strong significance in atopic dermatitis (log<sub>2</sub>FC = −0.50, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 5 × 10<sup>−3</sup>) and in asthma (log<sub>2</sub>FC = −0.05, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.06). *OIP5-AS1* was modestly but significantly downregulated in atopic dermatitis (log<sub>2</sub>FC = −0.19, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 8 × 10<sup>−3</sup>). Pooled meta-analysis confirmed nontrivial cross-disease signal for the comparators: *MIR22HG* pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC = +0.46 (*I*<sup>2</sup> = 97 %), *GABPB1-AS1* pooled log<sub>2</sub>FC = −0.10 (*I*<sup>2</sup> = 97 %). Against this background, *IDI2-AS1* alone returned the lowest pooled effect size (5.1 × 10<sup>−5</sup>) and, uniquely, near-zero between-study heterogeneity (*I*<sup>2</sup> = 0.26 %). The flatness of *IDI2-AS1* at the tissue-bulk level is therefore a property of this lncRNA specifically, not a generic feature of low-abundance lncRNAs in allergic tissue.

### 3.5 Specificity: the type-2 cytokine network is appropriately disease-stratified

A complementary specificity check on the cytokine side confirmed that the analysis captures known disease biology in the expected pattern (Figure 6). *IL13* was the strongest disease-discriminating cytokine in AD (log<sub>2</sub>FC = +4.92) and was also significantly elevated in EoE (Sherrill +0.91, Kleuskens +1.5), as expected for the canonical type-2 effector. *IL5* was significantly elevated in asthma (+0.42, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.014) and EoE-Sherrill (+0.61, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 0.002), consistent with eosinophil recruitment. *IL6* and *TNF* were strongly elevated in AD only, in line with that disease's broader inflammatory profile. *IFNG* was significantly downregulated in asthmatic nasal epithelium (−0.66, *p*<sub>adj</sub> = 5 × 10<sup>−7</sup>), consistent with the type-2 polarization of allergic airway disease. The cytokine panel therefore behaves as expected from established immunology, providing a positive control against which the specific flatness of *IDI2-AS1* in the same cohorts can be interpreted with confidence.
