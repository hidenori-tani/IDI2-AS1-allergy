Dear OUP Transfer Desk Assistant,

I am writing to seek clarification on the Open Access (OA) policy applicable to my manuscript, which was transferred from *Briefings in Bioinformatics* (BIB-26-1123) to *Briefings in Functional Genomics* (BFG) via the OUP Transfer Desk on 2026-05-19 and which I submitted via Manuscript Central on 2026-05-20.

**Manuscript details**
- Title: *A reusable cell-composition-adjusted re-analysis pipeline for interpreting bulk-tissue null results in low-abundance lncRNA studies: IDI2-AS1 / IL5 as a worked example across type-2 allergic diseases*
- Author: Hidenori Tani (single author), Yokohama University of Pharmacy, Japan
- Article type at submission: Primary Research
- Submitted to BFG: 2026-05-20

**Background to my inquiry**

When I accepted the OUP Transfer Desk offer on 2026-05-19, the BFG submission package I prepared explicitly indicated the **subscription publication route** in the cover letter and title page (no open access charge requested), based on my understanding at the time that BFG offered a subscription option.

After submission, I have now seen the BFG journal page noting that **Briefings in Functional Genomics became fully Open Access from 1 January 2025**, meaning all accepted manuscripts must publish under an OA license and are subject to an APC charge (https://academic.oup.com/bfg/pages/open-access).

This is a material change from my expectation at the time of accepting the transfer offer. I am a single-author researcher with **no specific funding for this work** (as declared in the manuscript Funding statement), and an APC at the standard OUP OA rate is not feasible for me.

**My questions**

1. **Transitional arrangement for transferred manuscripts.** Are manuscripts transferred via the OUP Transfer Desk from a subscription/hybrid-era originating journal (in my case, the originating decision at BiB was an editorial-screen decline on 2026-05-18 without external peer review) subject to the new BFG OA-only policy at the time of submission to BFG, or is there any transitional or grandfather provision for transferred submissions whose route was selected before the policy change was clear to the author?

2. **APC waiver availability.** If the OA policy applies in full, are APC waivers (full or partial) available for unfunded single-author researchers based at non-Research4Life-eligible institutions? I would appreciate guidance on the formal application route, eligibility criteria, and typical decision turnaround.

3. **Withdrawal pathway.** If neither a transitional arrangement nor a waiver is feasible, I would prefer to **withdraw the BFG submission before any editorial decision is taken**, so that I can resubmit elsewhere without the risk of incurring an APC. Could you confirm the correct procedure for withdrawing a transferred submission via Manuscript Central, and confirm whether withdrawal at this stage carries any restriction on resubmission to other publishers?

I would be grateful for guidance at your earliest convenience, as the timing of any withdrawal decision is most cleanly handled before the manuscript enters formal editorial review.

Thank you for your assistance.
