## Methods

### Ethics

This study used only publicly available, fully de-identified RNA-seq datasets obtained with appropriate ethical approval by the original investigators. No new human or animal data were generated, and no additional ethical approval was required.

### Dataset selection and acquisition

Public bulk-tissue RNA-seq datasets from human IL5-driven allergic diseases were identified by querying GEO and ArrayExpress in March 2026. Inclusion criteria were: (i) human disease tissue with case/control or externally comparable control samples; (ii) RNA-seq at sufficient depth to detect lncRNAs; (iii) availability of per-sample raw counts or FPKM; and (iv) detection of *IDI2-AS1* (ENSG00000260196) as a non-zero feature in the supplementary expression matrix. After applying these criteria, five datasets covering four allergic diseases were retained (Table 1): asthma (GSE152004, *n* = 695 nasal epithelial brushings; Sajuthi et al., 2020); atopic dermatitis (GSE121212, *n* = 65 skin biopsies; Tsoi et al., 2019); eosinophilic esophagitis (GSE246323, *n* = 10; Kleuskens et al., 2024; and GSE58640, *n* = 16; Sherrill et al., 2014); and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis (GSE136825, *n* = 70 nasal polyp and turbinate biopsies; Peng et al., 2019). Three additional candidate datasets (GSE201955, GSE65832, GSE179269) were excluded because *IDI2-AS1* was absent from the processed matrix. For GSE136825, only patient nasal-polyp biopsies (NP, *n* = 42) and healthy-donor inferior-turbinate biopsies (Control IT, *n* = 28) were used. We note that this particular contrast is confounded by anatomical site (polyp vs turbinate) and donor source (patient vs unrelated healthy donor) and is therefore not structurally matched in the way the other cohorts are; results from this cohort are interpreted with that caveat, and the quantitative decomposition does not rest on it. Sample-to-condition mapping is documented in `data/metadata/final_datasets.csv` and the per-dataset loader scripts. Inclusion criterion (iv) restricts the conclusions to cohorts in which *IDI2-AS1* is detectably expressed at the bulk level.

### Differential expression analysis

For the four raw-count datasets (GSE152004, GSE121212, GSE246323, GSE136825), low-count features were removed with the conventional DESeq2 pre-filter of total count ≥ 10 across all samples (Love et al., 2014), and differential expression was computed with DESeq2 (v1.42.1) under a single-factor design (`~ group`, control as reference) using the Wald test with Benjamini–Hochberg FDR correction. This pre-filter only removes features with essentially no information for dispersion estimation and is not the basis of any detection claim; *IDI2-AS1* was verified to pass the filter (i.e., to be detectably expressed) in all five retained cohorts prior to analysis, and *IL5* in four of them; *IL5* fell below the count threshold in the small GSE246323 cohort (*n* = 10) and is therefore excluded from that cohort's *IL5*-dependent steps (Supplementary Table 1). Detectable *IDI2-AS1* is the operative inclusion criterion. Because the threshold is a fixed total-count floor rather than a per-group or abundance-stratified cut, it is deliberately permissive for low-abundance transcripts; we note this as a design choice — a more stringent or cohort-size-adaptive filter would remove additional marginal features but does not change the retained focal-gene results, with the documented exceptions of features that are genuinely below threshold in a given cohort (*IL5* in GSE246323, and the a-priori comparator *LITATS1* in the symbol-keyed datasets). The cohorts as deposited do not carry uniformly harmonised covariate metadata (age and sex are absent or inconsistently coded across the five matrices), so the differential-expression and decomposition models condition on case/control status only; residual confounding by demographic covariates therefore cannot be excluded and is noted as a limitation. Effect sizes for the patient-vs-control contrast were stabilised by adaptive shrinkage with apeglm (Zhu et al., 2019; `lfcShrink(type = "apeglm")`), and the resulting log<sub>2</sub> fold-changes and posterior standard errors were used as the input statistics for downstream meta-analysis. For the FPKM dataset (GSE58640), expression was log<sub>2</sub>(FPKM + 1)-transformed and analysed with limma (v3.58.1; Ritchie et al., 2015) using the `limma-trend` workflow. A focal-gene panel covering *IDI2-AS1*, four comparator lncRNAs (*MIR22HG*, *GABPB1-AS1*, *OIP5-AS1*, *LITATS1* — previously identified by our group as stress-responsive in human cells; Abe et al., 2023; Yagi et al., 2024), and six type-2 / inflammatory cytokines (*IL5*, *IL4*, *IL13*, *IL6*, *TNF*, *IFNG*) was extracted from each per-dataset table; the full per-dataset tables are provided in `results/tables/`. *LITATS1* fell below the count threshold in all symbol-keyed datasets and is therefore reported only at the level of dataset-wise availability.

### Cross-disease meta-analysis

Per-dataset patient-vs-control log<sub>2</sub> fold-changes and standard errors for each focal gene were combined across cohorts using random-effects meta-analysis (REML, `metafor::rma`, v4.6-0; Viechtbauer, 2010). Heterogeneity was quantified with Cochran's *Q* and *I*<sup>2</sup>. Forest plots for *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* were generated with `metafor::forest` (Fig. 2). Ensembl-ID rows in the two Ensembl-keyed datasets (GSE246323 and GSE136825) were collapsed onto HGNC symbols prior to pooling; *IDI2-AS1* (ENSG00000260196) was available in both, whereas *IL5* (ENSG00000113525) contributed from GSE136825 only and was below the count threshold in GSE246323. *IDI2-AS1* was pooled across all five datasets (k = 5); *IL5* across four (k = 4; below threshold in GSE246323); the comparator lncRNAs across the three symbol-keyed datasets (typically k = 3). The differing number of contributing cohorts between *IDI2-AS1* (k = 5) and the comparator lncRNAs (k = 3) means the cross-gene contrast in pooled estimates is descriptive rather than a formally matched comparison (noted in Results and Limitations). One further caveat applies to the input statistics: the standard errors entering the random-effects model are apeglm-shrunk posterior SEs for the four DESeq2 cohorts and ordinary sampling SEs for the single limma-trend cohort (GSE58640). These are not identical in nature; because GSE58640 contributes a single small cohort and the qualitative pooled conclusion (a converging near-null for *IDI2-AS1*) is unchanged if it is excluded, we retain it but flag the mixed-SE pooling as a limitation.

### Sample-level correlation and cell-type-adjusted analysis

Within each dataset, per-sample *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* abundance was extracted from variance-stabilised counts (`DESeq2::vst(blind = TRUE)`) or from log<sub>2</sub>(FPKM + 1) values. Pairwise Spearman correlation was computed per dataset (Fig. 3; Table 2). To test whether bulk *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* covariation could be attributed to shared expression in allergic-effector cells, two compartment-specific marker-gene signatures were constructed from canonical eosinophil markers (CLC, EPX, PRG2, RNASE2, RNASE3, SIGLEC8, CCR3) and Th2 / type-2 markers (GATA3, IL4R, CCR4, PTGDR2, IL13, IL17RB). For each sample, a signature score was computed as the mean of per-gene *z*-scores, with each gene standardised across the samples *within* a single dataset (no cross-dataset standardisation), so that the score is a within-cohort relative-abundance index. Cell-type-adjusted Spearman partial correlation between *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* was then estimated with `ppcor::pcor.test` (Kim, 2015), conditioning on both signatures (Fig. 4 and Supplementary Fig. 1; Table 2).

For the largest cohort (GSE152004 asthma, *n* = 695), a bootstrap decomposition of the observed *IDI2-AS1*–*IL5* association was performed with `mediation::mediate` (v4.5.0; Tingley et al., 2014) using *IDI2-AS1* as the predictor, eosinophil abundance as the intermediate covariate, and *IL5* as the response in a linear model (1,000 nonparametric bootstrap resamples; percentile CIs), reporting the total association together with its composition-attributable and residual components. The eosinophil compartment was used as the sole intermediate covariate — rather than including Th2 in parallel — because eosinophils are the cellular endpoint of the IL5 axis and the compartment that most strongly attenuated the partial correlation. Because the data are cross-sectional and observational, these components are statistical partitions of the association under the assumed linear model, not counterfactual causal effects; this is stated explicitly wherever the decomposition is reported. The same decomposition was also run in the two next-largest cohorts (AD, *n* = 65; CRSwNP, *n* = 70) as an exploratory, underpowered check; the smaller EoE cohorts (*n* = 10, 16) fell below the *n* ≥ 20 threshold for stable bootstrap estimation and were not decomposed.

To test whether the cell-composition result depends on the marker-mean proxy, the partial correlation and the decomposition were repeated using an independent, transcriptome-wide deconvolution. Per-sample abundance of 64 cell types, including eosinophils, was estimated with xCell (Aran et al., 2017) from library-size-normalised expression (DESeq2 normalised counts for count datasets; FPKM for GSE58640), with Ensembl identifiers mapped to HGNC symbols via Ensembl BioMart. Because xCell's default spillover-compensation step floored the eosinophil estimate in the epithelial cohorts (driving ≈ 41 % of asthma samples to exactly zero with near-zero variance), we used the raw ssGSEA enrichment score — which retained a biologically plausible non-zero distribution — as the abundance estimate for this rare, spillover-prone cell type, and report the compensated score alongside it to document the artifact (Fig. 6). Concordance between the marker and xCell eosinophil estimates was assessed by Spearman correlation. Robustness of the decomposition to unmeasured intermediate–outcome confounding was assessed with `mediation::medsens` (Imai et al., 2010), which reports the residual correlation ρ at which the composition-attributable component would be nullified. CIBERSORTx-ready (Newman et al., 2019) CPM/FPKM input matrices are additionally provided in `results/intermediate/cibersortx_input/` for verification against the LM22 reference. Generative-AI use in preparing this work is disclosed in full in the Declarations.

### Specificity heatmaps

To place the *IDI2-AS1* result in the context of related lncRNAs and the type-2 cytokine network, two heatmaps were produced with ComplexHeatmap (v2.18.0; Gu et al., 2016) as Fig. 5A (lncRNAs) and 5B (cytokines). Per-dataset patient-vs-control log<sub>2</sub> fold-changes were assembled into a gene × dataset matrix, rows ordered with the focal gene on top, and a symmetric colour scale clipped at ±1 log<sub>2</sub> unit for the lncRNA panel (Fig. 5A, which spans the full observed range of those features) and at ±4 log<sub>2</sub> units for the cytokine panel (Fig. 5B, to accommodate the *IL13*-in-AD outlier).
