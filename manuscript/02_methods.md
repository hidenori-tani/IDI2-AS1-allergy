## 2. Materials and Methods

### 2.1 Dataset selection and acquisition

Public bulk-tissue RNA-seq datasets from human IL5-driven allergic diseases were identified by querying the NCBI Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO) and ArrayExpress in March 2026. Inclusion criteria were: (i) human disease tissue with paired or matched controls; (ii) RNA-seq (not microarray) at sufficient depth to detect long noncoding RNAs; (iii) availability of per-sample expression matrices (raw counts or FPKM) — datasets providing only pre-computed differential-expression tables were excluded; and (iv) detection of *IDI2-AS1* (ENSG00000260196) as a non-zero feature in the supplementary expression matrix. After applying these criteria, five datasets covering four allergic diseases were retained for analysis (Table 1): asthma (GSE152004, *n* = 695 nasal epithelial brushings; Sajuthi et al., 2020); atopic dermatitis (GSE121212, *n* = 65 skin biopsies; Tsoi et al., 2019); eosinophilic esophagitis (GSE246323, *n* = 10 esophageal biopsies, Kleuskens et al., 2024; and GSE58640, *n* = 16 esophageal biopsies, Sherrill et al., 2014); and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyposis (GSE136825, *n* = 70 nasal polyp and turbinate biopsies; Peng et al., 2019). Three additional candidate datasets (GSE201955, GSE65832, GSE179269) were excluded because *IDI2-AS1* was absent from the available processed matrix or because per-sample raw data could not be recovered without re-quantification from SRA. For GSE136825, only the patient nasal-polyp biopsies (NP, *n* = 42) and unrelated healthy-donor inferior-turbinate biopsies (Control IT, *n* = 28) were used; the within-patient paired turbinate samples (NP_IT, *n* = 33) were excluded so that case/control contrasts in this cohort were structurally comparable to those in the other four datasets. Sample-to-condition mapping was reconstructed for every dataset from the GEO series matrix metadata; assignments are documented in `data/metadata/final_datasets.csv` and the per-dataset loader scripts (`code/R/loaders/load_GSE*.R`).

### 2.2 Differential expression analysis

For the four raw-count datasets (GSE152004, GSE121212, GSE246323, GSE136825), low-count features (rowSum < 10) were removed and differential expression was computed with DESeq2 (v1.42.1; Love et al., 2014) under a single-factor design (`~ group`, control as reference) using the Wald test with Benjamini–Hochberg FDR correction. Effect sizes for the patient-vs-control contrast were stabilized by adaptive shrinkage with apeglm (Zhu et al., 2019; `lfcShrink(coef = "group_patient_vs_control", type = "apeglm")`), and the resulting log<sub>2</sub> fold-changes and posterior standard errors were used as the input statistics for downstream meta-analysis. For the FPKM dataset (GSE58640), expression was log<sub>2</sub>(FPKM + 1)-transformed and analyzed with limma (v3.58.1; Ritchie et al., 2015) using the `limma-trend` workflow (`lmFit` followed by `eBayes(trend = TRUE)`); coefficient standard errors were recovered as `stdev.unscaled[, 2] · sigma`. A focal-gene panel covering the lncRNA of interest (*IDI2-AS1*, ENSG00000260196), four comparator lncRNAs (*MIR22HG*, *GABPB1-AS1*, *OIP5-AS1*, *LITATS1* — selected because the same lncRNAs were previously identified by our group as bacterial- and viral-stress-responsive in human cells; Abe et al., 2023; Yagi et al., 2024) and six type-2 / inflammatory cytokines (*IL5*, *IL4*, *IL13*, *IL6*, *TNF*, *IFNG*) was extracted from each per-dataset table for figure construction; the full per-dataset DEG tables are provided in `results/tables/`.

### 2.3 Cross-disease meta-analysis

Per-dataset patient-vs-control log<sub>2</sub> fold-changes and standard errors for each focal gene were combined across the available cohorts using a random-effects meta-analysis (REML estimator, `metafor::rma`, v4.6-0; Viechtbauer, 2010). Heterogeneity was quantified with Cochran's *Q* and *I*<sup>2</sup>. Forest plots for *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* were generated with `metafor::forest` (Figure 2). The Ensembl-ID gene rows for *IDI2-AS1* (ENSG00000260196) and *IL5* (ENSG00000113525) — which appear in the two Ensembl-keyed datasets (GSE246323 and GSE136825) — were collapsed onto their HGNC symbols prior to pooling so that each gene contributes at most one effect estimate per dataset. Genes matched only by HGNC symbol (the comparator lncRNAs *MIR22HG*, *GABPB1-AS1*, *OIP5-AS1* and the cytokines *IL13*, *IL6*, *TNF*, *IFNG*, *IL4*) were pooled across the three symbol-keyed datasets in which they passed the count-filter (typically k = 3, with k = 2 for *IL4*, which was below the count threshold in atopic-dermatitis skin). *IDI2-AS1* was pooled across all five datasets (k = 5) by virtue of being matched on both its HGNC symbol and its Ensembl ID; *IL5* was pooled across four (k = 4; below threshold in GSE246323).

### 2.4 Sample-level correlation and cell-type-adjusted analysis

Within each dataset, per-sample *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* abundance was extracted from variance-stabilized counts (`DESeq2::vst(blind = TRUE)`, raw-count datasets) or from log<sub>2</sub>(FPKM + 1) values (GSE58640). Pairwise Spearman correlation between *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* was computed per dataset (Table 2). To test whether any tissue-bulk *IDI2-AS1*↔*IL5* covariation could be attributed to shared expression in allergic-effector cells, two compartment-specific marker-gene signatures were constructed from canonical eosinophil markers (CLC, EPX, PRG2, RNASE2, RNASE3, SIGLEC8, CCR3) and Th2 / type-2 markers (GATA3, IL4R, CCR4, PTGDR2, IL13, IL17RB). For each sample, a signature score was computed as the mean of the per-gene *z*-scores across the panel (per-dataset standardization). Cell-type-adjusted Spearman partial correlation between *IDI2-AS1* and *IL5* was then estimated with `ppcor::pcor.test` (Kim, 2015), conditioning on both signature scores. For the largest cohort (GSE152004 asthma, *n* = 695), a formal causal-mediation analysis was performed with `mediation::mediate` (v4.5.0; Tingley et al., 2014) using *IDI2-AS1* as treatment, the eosinophil signature score as mediator, and *IL5* as outcome (1,000 nonparametric bootstrap resamples; percentile confidence intervals). Mediator and outcome models were fit by ordinary least squares (`m_model: eos_score ~ idi2as1`; `o_model: il5 ~ idi2as1 + eos_score`), and the average causal mediation effect (ACME), average direct effect (ADE), and proportion mediated were extracted from the bootstrap distribution. The marker-signature scoring approach was chosen instead of CIBERSORTx (Newman et al., 2019) for full local reproducibility from the public count matrices; CIBERSORTx-ready CPM/FPKM input matrices are nonetheless provided in `results/intermediate/cibersortx_input/` for independent verification with the LM22 reference (`code/R/08_prepare_cibersortx_input.R`).

### 2.5 Specificity heatmaps

To place the *IDI2-AS1* result in the context of related lncRNAs and the broader type-2 cytokine network, two heatmaps were produced with ComplexHeatmap (v2.18.0; Gu et al., 2016). For each panel, per-dataset patient-vs-control log<sub>2</sub> fold-changes were assembled into a gene × dataset matrix; rows were ordered with the focal gene (*IDI2-AS1* or *IL5*) on top, columns were ordered by disease, and disease and total-*n* annotations were drawn above the heatmap. The color scale was symmetric around zero and clipped at ±4 log<sub>2</sub> units to keep the *IL13*-in-AD outlier (log<sub>2</sub>FC ≈ 4.9) from compressing the visible dynamic range of the rest of the panel.
